Interflora called attention to gender inequalities

Interflora brought attention to the persistent disparities between men and women on International Women's Day.

They released ‘The Equal Power’ flower bouquet which consisted of white and red roses that visually represented gender inequalities – the floral arrangement was used as a stark visualisation of just how often women are overlooked and outnumbered.

We were welcomed back to the bar with Guinness.

This year was no exception to the Irish stout company's reputation for creative marketing.

In order to encourage individuals to order a Guinness as their first in-pub pint, this campaign saw the reopening of several hospitality establishments in the United Kingdom.

The campaign's centerpiece was an emotive commercial that showed various pictures of how the nation might come to a standstill in 2020, with commonplace items standing in for the dramatic black-and-white contrast of a pint of Guinness. For instance, a white cat perched on a black trash can. all set to an emotive rendition of "Always On My Mind" by Elvis Presley.
